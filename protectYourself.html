Protect yourself

Precautionary measures to take in order to not get hacked!

Here's a short video summary on how to protect yourself!

Make sure your passwords are complex and long with both lowercase and uppercase letters, and include numbers. Avoid using auto-complete.

Don’t access any important information, like your finances, or just anything personal on public WiFi. It’s best to do those things on a more secure connection.

Install a security software, like an antivirus, or antimalware. Many are free, or have a free trial. For simple files, use VirusTotal.

Always keep your software up to date. Often, updates can include security enhancements.

Sign up for account alerts; a lot of email providers and websites will notify you when your account is accessed from an unusual location.

Some seemingly innocent USB drives can actually execute malicious code on your computer. For example, the USB Rubber Ducky, or the more recent Bash Bunny. There is also a process called Juice Jacking, in which malicious code is uploaded to your phone when you start charging it. As a rule of thumb, don't plug anything suspicious into your computer/phone.
